## Introduction
I was halfway through writing my notes on quantum electrodynamics when I realized I wanted to discuss the Dirac Equation in more depth. So here we are. 

The main idea is that the Schrödinger is well and good for non-relativistic quantum mechanics, but since it is not Lorentz invariant, it is inadequate for a relativistic theory. This is troubling unless your name is Paul Dirac and the year is 1928, in which case you decide to invent new math and change the trajectory of the next century of physics research.
