Other Interests

  • God Module V11, Horspens 40, AL
  • Stasis V11, Calico Basin, NV
  • Mega Man V11, Hospital Boulders, AL
  • Pocket Sands V10/11, Hospital Boulders, AL
  • 3 Pads and a Toddler V10 FA, Calico Basin, NV
  • Hustle and Flow V10, Hospital Boulders, AL

Clarinet Playing

Playing at a concert with the MIT Ribotones.
I have played the clarinet for 12 years, first with the Decatur High School Wind Ensemble and currently with the MIT Wind Ensemble. In high school, I played with the Georgia District Honour band, and more recently, I played for two years with the MIT Video Game Orchestra (VGO). I also occasionally play with chamber groups such as the MIT Ribotones, a group of MIT students who perform in retirement homes in the Boston and Cambridge area.
My favourite pieces include Crussel's Third Clarinet Concerto Op. 11, Busoni's Clarinet Concertino Op. 47, Cavallini's Adagio e Tarantella, and Weber's Clarinet Concertino Op. 26.

Board, War, and Role-Playing Games

My beloved 0-5 Seraphon.
In the little free time I do have, I enjoy playing games, particularly with the MIT Strategic Game Society (SGS). I mainly enjoyer heavier highly-interactive games and my favourites include Spirit Island, Oath, Caverna, Barrage, War of the Ring, Clash of Cultures, and of course, Twilight Imperium. I also played a good amount of Warhammer: Age of Sigmar back in high school, although I am less able to indulge my miniature-painting desires nowadays (although rumours have it, I am becoming interested in Trench Crusade). I have also recently begun exploring role-playing games, hosting a Dungeon World campaign for my friends over the last few months.

Origami

In middle and high school, I did a good amount of origami, even designing my own models. I did a few exhibitions at my high school and library, but these days I’m afraid I have not the time. Someday, I may go back to relearn some paper folding, but for now my works remain a pleasant memory of simpler, less busy times.
